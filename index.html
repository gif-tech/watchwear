Knowledge Base

Understanding Watch Movements

Every movement type has unique characteristics and maintenance requirements. Understanding yours is the first step to lifelong preservation.

⚙️

Mechanical Watches

Mechanical watches are powered entirely by a hand-wound mainspring — no battery, no electronics. They represent the pinnacle of horological craft, with hundreds of intricate components working in precise harmony. Because they rely on fine oils and exact tolerances, mechanical watches require professional servicing every 3 to 5 years. A full service includes disassembly, ultrasonic cleaning, re-oiling, and regulation to ensure continued accuracy and long-term movement health.

Service Interval: Every 3–5 Years  |  Power Source: Hand-Wound Mainspring
🔋

Quartz Watches

Quartz watches use an electronic oscillator regulated by a quartz crystal to deliver exceptional timekeeping accuracy — typically within ±15 seconds per month. The primary maintenance need is a battery replacement every 1 to 3 years. During a battery change, a watchmaker should also inspect gaskets to preserve water resistance. A full movement service is typically recommended every 8 to 10 years to replenish dried lubricants and inspect capacitors.

Service Interval: Battery every 1–3 Yrs  |  Power Source: Battery Cell
🌀

Automatic Watches

Automatic (self-winding) watches build on mechanical technology by incorporating a freely rotating rotor that harvests kinetic energy from the wearer's wrist movement to continuously wind the mainspring. This eliminates the need for hand-winding during regular wear. Like all mechanical movements, automatics depend on precision lubrication and should receive a professional service every 4 to 6 years to maintain optimal performance and protect the rotor and escapement components.

Service Interval: Every 4–6 Years  |  Power Source: Rotor + Mainspring
☀️

Solar Watches

Solar-powered watches convert both natural and artificial light into electrical energy, stored in a long-lasting rechargeable power cell. This makes them exceptionally low-maintenance for daily wearers. The power cell typically lasts 10 to 15 years before replacement is needed. Aside from periodic crystal and gasket inspection, solar watches require minimal intervention. It is advisable to avoid prolonged storage in darkness, as the reserve can deplete if the watch is not exposed to light regularly.

Service Interval: Cell every 10–15 Yrs  |  Power Source: Solar / Light Energy
